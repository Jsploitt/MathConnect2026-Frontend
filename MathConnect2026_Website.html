About the Conference

Where Mathematics
Meets Application

MathConnect 2026 marks the second edition of the MathConnect conference series, following the successful inaugural meeting held two years ago. The conference provides a dynamic forum where mathematicians, researchers, and practitioners can present and discuss recent advances across a broad spectrum of mathematical sciences.

A central objective is to strengthen the dialogue between fundamental mathematical research and its applications in science, engineering, finance, and emerging technological domains. By bringing together experts from diverse mathematical traditions, MathConnect 2026 stimulates exchange of ideas and promotes new perspectives at the interface between theory and practice.

The conference also fosters interdisciplinary collaborations and encourages exploration of emerging research directions, while inspiring early-career researchers and students through exposure to leading scholars.

Scientific Program

Conference Themes

The scientific program is organized around thematic directions reflecting the depth of modern mathematical research and its growing interdisciplinary reach.

Graphs, Combinatorics & Cryptography

Discrete structures, algorithmic thinking, and information security intersect. Research spanning structural combinatorics, complex networks, and cryptographic theory.

Analytical & Modelling Applications

Analytical methods for describing complex systems. Mathematical modelling translating real-world phenomena into rigorous frameworks for qualitative and quantitative insight.

Linear Algebra & Applications

Spectral theory, matrix analysis, numerical algorithms, and large-scale computation. Algebraic concepts evolving to address high-dimensional and data-intensive challenges.

Stochastic Processes & Applications

Probabilistic modelling and the mathematical study of uncertainty - indispensable in finance, physics, biology, and engineering.

ML, Optimization, Statistics & Data Analytics

Mathematical foundations of learning algorithms, optimization techniques, and statistical inference driving modern AI and data science.

Abstract Submission Guidelines

  • An abstract of no more than 500 words should be submitted to the conference secretariat by the deadline.
  • Acceptance of abstracts will be announced after refereeing within two weeks after submission.
  • Source files of abstracts and full-length papers should be prepared in LaTeX.
  • Submissions must be original and unpublished work not under review elsewhere.
  • All submissions will undergo peer review by the Scientific Committee.
  • Abstracts should clearly state the research question, methodology, and main results.
  • At least one author of each accepted abstract must register for the conference.

About KFUPM

King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals is one of the leading universities in the Arab world and a premier destination for research in science, engineering, and mathematics. The campus in Dhahran offers world-class facilities for academic conferences.

Weather in December

Dhahran in December enjoys mild, pleasant weather - ideal for visiting. Expect temperatures between 12-24°C with little to no rainfall.
